Live Dublin Like a Local

We keep you up to date with events, tales and things to do around the city so you can experience
Dublin like a local.

Pablo Picante - 4 Clarendon Market

Whatever your reason for visiting Dublin, visiting Pablo Picante is a must. Leading the way for the Burrito frenzy within the city, "Pablo's" will satisfy not only the most hardcore spice enthusiast but any flavour seeking tourist. With large portion sizes and moderate prices this is a must visit. We at Wildewalkingtours recommend visiting Clarendon Market, just off Grafton Street, where you will find their Burrito Bar and new Tortas restaurant. Why not grab a burrito and beer and sit outside and relax in the sun. Your tastebuds will not be disappointed. 

Mary’s - 8 Wicklow St, Dublin 2

Mary’s is a rare and unique gem situated in the heart of one of Dublin’s busiest districts. Doubling as a hardware store and with a Wow Burger downstairs it has something for everyone. Drinking in Mary’s is like stepping back in time and finding yourself in a small rural Irish pub. The décor is quirky and rustic , appealing to both the student crowd and the mature drinker. If you’re visiting Dublin it’s a great place to experience a more traditional pub atmosphere and listen to some traditional Irish music (every Tuesday & Thursday evening). They have a great collection of drinks and if you’re feeling peckish you only need to go downstairs and get a delicious burger from the bars very own Wow Burger.
Mary’s is the only place in Dublin you can buy a beer, a burger and a 35 Watt Philips light bulb at the same time. 

Father Pat Noise Plaque

“He died under suspicious circumstances when his carriage plunged into the Liffey on August 10th 1919,”


In 2004 this bronze plaque was erected by an unknown individual(s) on O’Connell’s Bridge and went unnoticed for 2 years until a journalist at the Irish Tribune, Eoghan Rice brought it to the attention of the Dublin City Council. Debunked by Irish historian Pat Liddy as a hoax the plaque soon found itself a celebrity. Dubliners and tourists alike would take photos and leave floral tributes to this fictional priest. While it's still unknown who put it there the rumour goes that it was created by two brothers as a tribute to their father. The name Fr.Pat Noise may be a play on the Latin phrase “Pater Noster”, which means “Our Father”.
A labour councillor, Dermot Lacy proposed a motion in 2006 to allow the plaque to remain on the bridge permanently referring to it as “a bit of madness, a bit of colour”. While the council agreed to this, the plaque was removed in 2007 but was quickly replaced, assumedly by the same pranksters, and it remains there ever since.
